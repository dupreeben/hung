At Disneyland, on business

Hi. I'm Ben.

I'm a writer interested in the American west and Japan across literary and dystopian narratives.

Presently, I'm working on short stories of various shapes and sizes. I was most recently published in Cirque: A Literary Journal for Alaska and the Pacific Northwest, which featured my story I found you.

In 2014, I wrote Sarin Sunset, a novel centered on two people whose lives were turned upside-down by the 1995 Tokyo sarin gas attacks.

In a past life, I was a strategist and copy writer for U.S. Senators, members of Congress, mayors, non-profits, and candidates for public office across America.

I live in Portland, Oregon with my wife Mele and our daughter Sage. My joys include riding my road bike as fast as I can, watching sumo wrestling, frequenting good tiki bars and coffee shops, staring wistfully into nature, my two cats, and obsessively rooting for the Los Angeles Dodgers.
